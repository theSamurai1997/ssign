# Curation audit (2026-06-08)

Five background verification agents (one per SS type) audited every
row of the agent-curated tables in `data/curated/`. Per row they
checked:

(a) Does the cited DOI resolve to a real paper?
(b) Does that paper actually name this protein as a substrate of this SS?
(c) Is the locus_tag correct vs the source genome's NCBI annotation?
(d) Is the source organism documented to have the named SS AND at
    least one substrate of that SS?

Output TSVs in this directory preserve all original columns and add
two: `verification_status` (VERIFIED / PARTIAL / FAIL / NEEDS_REVIEW)
and `verification_notes` (one-line explanation per row).

## Cross-cutting patterns

### Fabricated or wrong DOIs (largest issue)

The 2026-06-08 curation pass produced systematically broken citations.
The pattern is most severe on T1SS, T4SS, and T6SS (~60% of unique
DOIs wrong or 404 on each), milder on T2SS, smallest on T3SS (~2%).

Concrete examples flagged by the agents:

- **T1SS**: 11 of 22 unique DOIs resolve to unrelated papers. Cited AprA DOI is actually Scott 1992 "CooB/CS1 pilin"; cited HasA DOI is an EMBO J "Note added" placeholder; cited LtxA DOI is McClain 2000 "Helicobacter VacA"; cited SiiE DOI is Plaut 2008 "Pertussis toxin". 5 more return 404. Only 6 of 22 are correct: Felmlee 1985 HlyA, Akatsuka 1997 Serralysin/HasA/PrtA, Awram & Smit 1998 RsaA, Hinsa & O'Toole 2003 LapA, Schaller 1999 ApxIVA, Linhartova 2010 RTX review.
- **T4SS**: ~60% of 75 unique DOIs broken. LnaB cited DOI is a Gondwanan tetrapod paleontology paper. MavN cited DOI is a planaria regeneration paper. Beps cited DOI 404s. Brucella Bsp series cites a Streptococcus pilus paper (correct is Myeni 2013, `10.1371/journal.ppat.1003556`). NyxA/B cite wrong paper (correct is Louche 2023, `10.1038/s41467-022-35763-8`).
- **T6SS**: multiple wrong citations. Tse6 cites Dellisanti instead of Whitney 2015 Cell. VgrG5_BT DOI doesn't resolve (real is IAI.01368-13). Tse6_BT_Mn references P. aeruginosa Tse6 paper rather than a BT-specific characterization.
- **T2SS**: 4 unique broken DOIs cascade through ~12 rows: `10.1146/annurev-micro-102215-095506` (no such article), `10.1128/JB.00146-13` (404; real pelN paper is `10.1128/jb.02118-12`), `10.1128/IAI.00388-15` (404; real StmPr paper is `10.1128/IAI.00672-15`), `10.1128/IAI.06031-11` (resolves to unrelated Campylobacter paper).
- **T3SS**: Only 1 wrong unique DOI: `10.1371/journal.pone.0040104` (cited on CT005, CT006) actually resolves to El-Manzalawy 2012 "BEST B-cell epitope prediction." The big T3SS DOIs (Jennings 2017 SPI-2, Tobe 2006 PNAS EHEC inventory, Peeters 2013 Ralstonia, Munkvold 2009 P. syringae Hops) are all correct and on-topic.

**Rescue path:** the proteins, organisms, locus_tags, and instance
bindings are correct in the vast majority of these rows; only the
`primary_ref` column is wrong. A re-citation pass (one agent per
unique broken DOI) can repoint citations without dropping data.
Estimated 270+ rows recoverable this way.

### UniProt accession unreliability

The `uniprot` column is systematically broken in T2SS (only ~25 of 101
correct mappings) and partially broken in T6SS. The error pattern
looks like a buggy bulk gene-name lookup that returned wrong-organism
or wrong-protein entries. Examples:

- T2SS Q9KSP5 "chiA1" → V. cholerae EAL protein (wrong)
- T2SS Q5ZRW3 "proA" → CorB transporter (wrong)
- T2SS P15524 "lasA" → Chlorobaculum csmA (wrong organism entirely)
- T2SS B2FT85 "stmPr3" → different Smlt protein
- T6SS Q9KNF7 "VasH" is at VC_A0007 not VC_A0117
- T6SS Q9KS44 "Hcp-1" is at VC_1417 but annotated DUF4123, not Hcp
- T6SS Q9KNE6 "Hcp-2" is at VC_A0019 but is VasW, not Hcp
- T4SS Q5ZXQ7 listed as RalF_Lp (lpg1950) but actually lpg0674 (adenylate cyclase)
- T4SS Q5ZTL4 listed as MvcA (lpg2148) but actually MavC (lpg2147)
- T4SS O25257 listed as CagF (HP_0543) but is Cag1 (HP_0520)

**Rescue path:** rebuild the `uniprot` column from `locus_tag` via
UniProt REST API. Locus_tag is largely trustworthy; one query per
(organism, locus_tag) pair returns the correct accession.

### Biology misclassifications (NOT rescuable)

These are real errors caught by the audit and must drop, not
re-cite. About 50 rows total.

**T6SS apparatus-as-effector confusion (~35 rows):**
Hcp, VgrG, and PAAR proteins are structural components of the T6SS
firing mechanism, not secreted substrates. Every `Hcp*`, `VgrG*`, and
`PAAR*` row in the T6SS table is flagged PARTIAL or FAIL by the
agent. Exceptions kept as VERIFIED: VgrG-3 (VC_A0123) and VgrG2b
(PA0262), which are "evolved VgrGs" with C-terminal effector domains
fused on.

**T6SS immunity / adaptor misclassification (~7 rows):**
TsiV1, TsiH, TsiV2, Tldi1, Tai, Tdi1/2, EagR1/2 are immunity proteins
or T6SS adaptors, not effectors.

**T2SS Type-1-as-Type-2 misclassification (4 rows):**
prtA, prtB, prtC, prtG in Dickeya are claimed as Out (T2SS)
substrates but in Dickeya these serralysins are secreted by the
PrtDEF Type 1 SS. The PrtDEF system is biochemically distinct from
the Out T2SS.

**T6SS wrong instance bindings (~5 rows):**
- TseT_H1 at PA0820 is misassigned; TseT is actually a T6SS-H2 effector at PA3907 per Burkinshaw 2018.
- BT chr-II rows (TseM_Mn_binding at BTH_II1883; RbsB at BTH_II1884) are mis-labeled T6SS-1, which is on chr I, not chr II.
- Brucella Bsp locus_tag swaps per Myeni 2013 (BspA at BAB1_1671 in the table is actually BspE per Myeni).

**T4SS biology calls (2 rows):**
- RARP-1: Kaur 2012 showed TolC-dependent (T1SS-style) secretion, not VirB.
- HGE-14: P44/MSP2 surface-protein family, not classically T4SS.

**T6SS hard FAILs (3 rows):**
- RhsP2 PA3905: RhsP2 is PA14_43100 in PA14, no PAO1 ortholog. PA3905 is tecT (chaperone).
- PoreEffector_BT BTH_II2127: cited DOI is a Serratia preprint, not Burkholderia; BTH_II2127 is on chr II, not in T6SS-1.
- Hcp-2 VC_A0019 (Aux2): UniProt Q9KNE6 is VasW (accessory), not Hcp; canonical Hcp-2 is at VC_A0017.

**T1SS hard FAILs (9 rows):**
- 5 eukaryotic-host TrEMBL artifacts: PLEKHF1 Homo sapiens, Plekhf1 Mus musculus, frpA in Dictyostelium discoideum, Cavenderia fasciculata, Heterostelium pallidum. These cannot host a bacterial T1SS.
- 4 Arsenophonus nasoniae rows: A. nasoniae genome literature documents T3SS only, no T1SS.

**T3SS hard FAILs (2 rows):**
- CT005 + CT006 cite `10.1371/journal.pone.0040104` which is a B-cell epitope paper, not a Chlamydia T3SS paper. Proteins may still be legitimate Chlamydia T3SS predictions but the citation is mismatched.

### Instance bindings (the load-bearing labels)

For training a classifier that must predict protein -> specific SS
instance, instance bindings are the most important labels. The audit
confirmed these are mostly correct across all 5 types:

- T1SS: single-instance (organism-level), no instance binding required.
- T2SS: every named instance (Eps, Lsp, Out, Xcp, Hxc, Xps, Exe, Pul, Yts1, Txc) confirmed present in its source organism.
- T3SS: multi-cluster splits (SPI-1/SPI-2, LEE + 14 prophage IDs, Ysc-Yop + Ysa, T3SS-1/T3SS-2 in V. parahaemolyticus) all validated against the cited literature.
- T4SS: instance bindings strong. All 8 Bartonella Beps correctly on VirB-Bep (none drifted to Trw). All Agrobacterium canonical Vir effectors correctly on VirB-Ti (not Trb/AvhB). Brucella single-VirB consistent. Legionella DotIcm consistent.
- T6SS: PAO1 H1/H2/H3 bindings nearly all correct (Tse1-7 on H1; PldA/Tle5a/Tle1/Tle3/Tla3/Tle4/VgrG2a/VgrG2b/TplE on H2; PldB/TseF/Azu on H3). One instance error: TseT_H1 should be H2 (see above).

This is the strongest finding of the audit: the protein-to-system-instance
labels survive the curation errors. For Tier-2 instance-aware
training the data is structurally sound; only the metadata (citations,
UniProt accessions) needs repair.

## Reproducibility

The agent-authored helper scripts in `agent_scripts/`
(`verify_t1ss.py`, `verify_t3ss.py`, `verify_t6ss.py`,
`build_t2ss_verified.py`, `build_verified.py`, `unique_dois.txt`)
encode the per-row verdicts the agents made. Re-running them against
the original `data/curated/` tables should reproduce these verified
TSVs (modulo any UniProt or NCBI changes since 2026-06-08).
